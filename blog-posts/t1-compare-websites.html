Technical Blog: My Favorite Websites
August 11, 2014
It's really not as easy as I thought to come up with a list of my favorite websites. I guess the best way to approach the question is to figure out what sites I spend the most time on. The easy winner there is Reddit.com. Reddit: When I say that I spend the most time on this website, I don't think you understand what I mean. I'm going to make a wild guess and say around 80% of my internet traffic is made up on reddit. The thing about reddit is that it doesn't look like anything special at first glance. In fact it's just a simplistic layout of links. The driver of reddit's popularity is the content. Reddit is so dense with user specific information that one can loose hours of time on the site without realizing it. Reddit brings everything that someone could be interested in right to one page. There is almost no point in ever looking at another site! Reddit does sell gold (a form of currency to tell users you like their post) but it is really just a way to ask for donations. But again, visually, there are no bells and whistles, just a simplistic site that's easy to navigate. CNN.com CNN is probably my 2nd most visited web pages. CNN isn't even really my favorite news source, but I find their website more intuitive than most of the other news channels. Everything is easy to see and put directly in front of your face. There is no need to search for anything. You can jump on the site, scan for new news and be off within just a few minutes. Nothing about the site really jumps out at me as beautiful or extremely creative, it is just easy to use. ...I'm starting to see a trend in my website picks. The site doesn't sell anything but add space, but they sell plenty of it. In fact that would probably be my main issue with the site. It is a bit too packed with adds, especially video adds. Chess.com Chess.com has to go on my list of favorite sites because I am always on it. However, the website itself is not that great looking. In fact, I really don't like it. It's clunky and I can get lost tryign to navigate. Maybe when I am done with DBC I can give them some help and make the site a bit more user friendly. So, when it comes down to it, it looks like my main influence in spending time on a site is its content. Apart from that I look for simplistic design and user friendly navigation. I don't really get caught up in catchy animations or overly dramatic layouts. Well I am off! See you next time. -John Williams
