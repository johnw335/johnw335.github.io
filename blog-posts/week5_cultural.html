The Benifits of Pairing
September 7th, 2014

When I joined DBC I had no idea how important working with others would be. I usually work alone and thought that pairing would be a minor inconvenience.

Little did I know that pairing would be such a great experience. One of the most frustrating parts of coding can be getting stuck on one issue for long periods of time.

Pairing adds another set of eyes and another creative brain to challenge. It allows a group to bounce ideas off one another and build something stronger than either person would have created individually.

That is one thing that I find particularly enjoyable about pairing. Seeing a solution that never would have crossed my mind helps me understand the code in a much more well rounded fashon.

Now let's talk about feedback!

I the the DBC community is pretty good about leaving positive and constructive feedback. I haven't been left any that left me discouraged or upset. One common thread in the feedback about me is that I need to me more vocal about my problem solving process. This isn't too surprising to me, and I know it's something that I need to work on.

The only difficult thing about writing feedback has been finding something to criticize. I have only paired with really enthusiastic smart people and have loved every session.

Pairing and feedback have both been very helpful in my growth as a coder. I can't believe how much I have gained from pairing especially.

Well i'm off to leave some feedback...
